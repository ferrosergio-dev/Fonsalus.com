⚡ Good news! Registering a company in Estonia is intentionally simple and can be done 100% online by yourself through the official e-Business Register. This guide shows you how – no intermediary needed.

How to register an Estonian OÜ yourself

Everything you need to know, straight from the official sources

📋 Quick comparison: Private Limited Company (OÜ)

Requirement Details
Minimum capital €0.01 (no minimum requirement since 01.02.2023)
State fee €265 (fast-track online) / €200 (standard)
Processing time 1-5 working days (next day for fast-track)
Founders Individuals or legal entities (any nationality)
Management At least one board member (can be non-resident)
Liability Shareholders are not personally liable for company obligations (ÄS § 135)

⚠️ Important note: If you choose share capital below €2,500 and the company becomes insolvent, shareholders may be liable for the difference between the actual capital and €2,500 to cover the costs of insolvency proceedings (based on Äriseadustik and court practice).

🔹 Step 1: Get e-Residency (if you're not an EU resident)

If you don't live in the EU, you need an e-Resident's digital ID to sign documents online.

  • What is it? A digital identity issued by Estonia that allows you to sign documents and use e-services remotely.
  • Cost: €150 (state fee from 01.01.2025)
  • Processing: Decision within 30 days, document ready in 2-5 weeks
  • Collection: You must pick it up in person at an Estonian embassy or Police and Border Guard Board service point
  • Apply here: eresident.politsei.ee

Note: e-Residency does NOT give you citizenship, residence rights, or automatically open a bank account.

🔹 Step 2: Prepare the required information

Before you start the registration, have these ready:

  • Company name: Check availability via the e-Business Register name search
  • Main activity (EMTAK code): Official classification
  • Shareholders: Names, birth dates, contact details, and share distribution (minimum share: €0.01 per shareholder)
  • Board members: At least one (can be the same as shareholder)
  • Share capital amount: You decide (minimum €0.01, must be paid before registration)
  • Registered address: A physical address in Estonia (can be your home or a virtual office service)

🔹 Step 3: Register online (two options)

Option A: Fast-track via e-Business Register portal (recommended)

  • Use the official portal: ariregister.rik.ee
  • Use the template articles of association (tüüppõhikiri) – no notary needed
  • All founders and board members must digitally sign the application
  • State fee: €265 (paid online by card)
  • Processing: next working day
  • If share capital is below €50,000: board members simply confirm payment
  • If share capital is above €50,000: bank confirmation required

Option B: Via notary

  • Useful if you cannot digitally sign or have complex ownership structures
  • Notary prepares the foundation agreement and articles of association
  • Additional notary fees apply (depends on share capital and number of founders)
  • State fee: €200 (paid by the notary on your behalf)

✅ After registration, you'll receive:

  • Company registration number
  • e-Business Register extract (official proof of incorporation)
  • Ability to register for VAT immediately (if needed)
  • Access to all e-services with your digital ID

📌 What's next? (post-registration checklist)

  • Register for VAT if your annual turnover will exceed €40,000 – Tax and Customs Board
  • Open a bank account – contact banks directly; they have their own KYC procedures
  • Register employees in the employment register on their first working day
  • Submit beneficial ownership information to the e-Business Register (required by RahaPTS § 76-77)
  • Prepare annual reports each financial year
